Planned Feature Design: The Event Card Deck
We are completely replacing the standard 2D6 dice roll with the official Catan Event Cards system (from the Traders & Barbarians expansion). This introduces a mathematically perfect 36-card deck, ensuring smooth resource distribution while adding dynamic events.
Card Distribution (36 Number Cards)
The deck perfectly mirrors dice probabilities:
•	2: 1 card | 3: 2 cards | 4: 3 cards | 5: 4 cards | 6: 5 cards
•	7: 6 cards (Activates Robber)
•	8: 5 cards | 9: 4 cards | 10: 3 cards | 11: 2 cards | 12: 1 card
The Events
Each card drawn triggers a specific board event before resource production:
•	Robber Attack (x6): Found on all "7" cards. Standard robber rules apply (discard half if >7 cards, move robber, steal).
•	Earthquake (x1): All players with at least one road must turn one sideways (no longer counts toward Longest Road). Costs 1 Lumber + 1 Brick to repair.
•	Epidemic (x1): Cities produce only 1 resource instead of 2 for the turn.
•	Good Neighbors (x1): Every player must pass 1 resource card to the player on their left.
•	Calm Seas (x1): Player(s) with the most harbor settlements/cities receives 1 free resource of their choice.
•	Tournament (x1): Player(s) with the most revealed Knight cards receives 1 free resource of their choice.
•	Trade Advantage (x1): The Longest Road holder may steal 1 resource from any player.
•	Conflict (x1): The Largest Army holder may steal 1 resource from any player.
•	No Event (x16): Peaceful turns where players simply collect resources based on the number.
The 37th Card: "New Year" (Anti-Card Counting)
To prevent players from mathematically calculating the exact remaining rolls in the late game, a New Year card is utilized.
•	Mechanic: During setup, 5 cards are dealt face-down. The New Year card is placed on top of them, followed by the remaining 31 cards. When the New Year card is drawn, the entire 36-card deck is immediately reshuffled. The bottom 5 cards are never seen.

The Friendly Robber Variant
The Friendly Robber is an official variant introduced in the Catan: Traders & Barbarians expansion. It was designed to prevent players from being aggressively targeted or crippled by bad luck at the very beginning of the game, allowing everyone a chance to get their initial engine running.
Here is the exact definition and the mechanical rules of the Friendly Robber:
The Core Rule: When a 7 is rolled or a Knight development card is played, the active player cannot move the robber to any terrain hex that is adjacent to a settlement owned by a player who has exactly 2 Victory Points (their starting score).
Mechanical Breakdown: To implement this correctly in a digital format, here are the specific nuances of the rule:
•	The Discard Phase Remains Unchanged: If a 7 is rolled, all players with more than 7 cards in their hand must still discard half, regardless of their Victory Point score. The "friendly" protection only applies to where the robber physically lands.
•	Knight Cards are Included: The protection applies whether the robber is being moved by a dice roll of 7 or by a player activating a Knight card.
•	Hidden Victory Points: Victory Point Development Cards (which are kept hidden face-down) do not count toward this threshold until they are revealed. If a player visibly has 2 VP on the board but secretly holds a VP card, the game still treats them as having 2 VP, meaning they are protected.
•	The Desert Fallback: In the highly unusual scenario where a 7 is rolled and every single non-desert hex on the board is protected by a 2-VP player, the robber is simply moved back to the Desert hex, and no resources are blocked or stolen.
Once a player builds their first new settlement or upgrades to a city (bringing their visible score to 3 VP), they lose their "friendly" protection for the rest of the game and become a valid target for the robber.

Refactoring 4 – Backward-Compatible Constructor Delegation (BoardDataInputs):
Before: BoardDataInputs had a 10-parameter constructor. Adding the fishing grounds stream as a required parameter would have broken all 500+ existing tests.
After: Added an 11-parameter constructor with the fishingGroundsStream. The original 10-parameter constructor delegates to the new one with null. The null propagates through to setupFishingGrounds(), which skips initialization when the stream is null.
Test: All 500+ pre-existing tests pass unchanged because they use the 10-parameter constructor.

Milestone 3 – Lessons Learned

1. Existing infrastructure matters: Before diving into implementation, the AI explored the entire codebase and found that 4 of the 8 fish-related classes already existed but were never connected to the game loop. This saved significant time — instead of designing the token system from scratch, we wired up the existing classes and focused on the missing pieces (terrain types, fishing grounds data, distribution logic, spending actions).

2. Characterization tests are the safety net: Writing 63 characterization tests before changing spendFishTokens' return type and fixing the cost values gave me confidence that the changes were correct. The tests caught the cost swap (FREE_ROAD was 7 instead of 5, DEV_CARD was 5 instead of 7) that would have gone unnoticed in manual testing.

3. Backward compatibility through constructor delegation: The pattern of adding an N+1 parameter constructor that the N-parameter constructor delegates to (with null for the new parameter) is invaluable. It let us add the fishing grounds stream without touching 500+ existing tests.

4. Fakes beat mocks for complex state: EasyMock strict mocks require configuring every expected method call in order, which becomes brittle for complex interactions. The hand-rolled FakeFishTokenSupplier with its queue and returned list was simpler to write, easier to read, and more powerful for sensing.

5. AI tool evaluation: Claude Opus 4.6 was highly effective at codebase exploration (finding the pre-existing fish classes), identifying Feathers patterns to apply, and generating comprehensive test suites. It was particularly good at diagnosing test failures — the strict mock and niceMock issues were identified and fixed in a single pass. The main area where I needed to guide the AI was on the domain rules (exact fish costs, Old Boot mechanics) where the spec needed to be provided explicitly.
